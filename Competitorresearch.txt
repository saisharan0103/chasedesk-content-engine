

Yes buddy. I researched the space. ChaseDesk has **two competitor groups**:

1. **Direct competitors** — receipt capture, OCR, invoice capture, Xero posting  
2. **Adjacent competitors** — client portals / document request / “stop chasing clients” platforms

The key insight: **nobody is positioning exactly like ChaseDesk yet**. Most competitors say “capture documents / automate bookkeeping.” ChaseDesk can own the sharper pain: **“stop chasing clients for missing receipts.”**

This is a big one.

Datamolino writes pages like:

That catches high-intent buyers already searching for tools. ChaseDesk should copy this.

You need pages like:

## 7. My honest competitor conclusion

ChaseDesk should not fight Dext on OCR.

Dext is too strong there.

ChaseDesk should fight on this:

> **Dext processes receipts after clients send them. ChaseDesk gets clients to send them in the first place.**

That is the wedge.

That is your differentiation.

Dext = capture and process.  
Hubdoc = store and extract.  
Client Hub = broad client portal.  
Financial Cents = broad workflow/client tasks.  
**ChaseDesk = receipt chasing autopilot for Xero firms.**

That is the cleanest position.

Yes buddy — ChaseDesk is **not only OCR**. It is mainly **follow-up automation + missing receipt collection + Xero posting**. That matters because most competitors talk about “processing documents,” while your sharper angle is **getting the client to actually send the damn document first**.
